My Recovery from Shoulder Replacement Surgery

Juggling healing, school, and mental health as a full time student

This website will document my recovery from shoulder surgery. As every person is different and has different expectations, this is not intended to be a end all document for anyone going through a similar surgery.
It started as a class project to create a website and has evolved as a sort a therapy for myself to look inward on how I am handling a difficult situation.
For a more detailed look at what this surgery consists of click here Mayo Clinic Shoulder Replacement Procedure

Background

I am a 43 year old father of 3 beautiful girls and husband to a very beautiful and understanding wife!
My decision to go back to school after 40 mirrors my reasons that I have to have surgery, I put things off for too long!
Early in my life I felt like I had to work whatever job I could find to support myself and then my family,
after that I didn't feel like I had that time to go back to school or deal with small nagging injuries.

Interests

I've always been a fan of video games, and nerd culture. But recently I've redisocvered my passion for photography.
The ability to capture moments in time that others would walk past is amazing to me, I hope I can develop my skills to share with more people.

Goals

My goal is to utilize what I learn durning my time at Olympic College to pursue a job that blends web design and photography.
Because of my health and family commitments my goal I plan on pursuing mostly gig work that allows me to set my own schedule.
