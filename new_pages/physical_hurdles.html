My Recovery from Shoulder Replacement Surgery

Juggling healing, school, and mental health as a full time student

This website will document my recovery from shoulder surgery. As every person is different and has different expectations, this is not intended to be a end all document for anyone going through a similar surgery.
It started as a class project to create a website and has evolved as a sort a therapy for myself to look inward on how I am handling a difficult situation.
For a more detailed look at what this surgery consists of click here Mayo Clinic Shoulder Replacement Procedure

The physical hurdles I've experienced balancing recovery and school:

Right after surgery I was given instructions on how to get in and out of my shoulder sling safely.
I was also given a limited list of recovery excercieses that I could do on my own in preperation for physical therapy.
The protective bandage was allowed to be removed 48 hours after surgery allowing for showers and inspection of the incision to make sure everything looked ok.
Sleeping has been difficult as trying to be comfortable with the shoulder sling leads to feelings of being restrained. That and my inability to roll on my side as made sleep harder than I thought it would be.

Limited movement outside of the sling and doing rehab to increase shoulder stength and mobility.

More movement allowed without the use of a sling. Weight restrictions generally removed, rehab as nessesary.
